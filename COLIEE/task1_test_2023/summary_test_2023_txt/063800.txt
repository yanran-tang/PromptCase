Applicant a veteran who served in the Canadian Armed Forces seeks judicial review of the decision of the Veterans Review and Appeal Board regarding his entitlement to a one-fifth pension for hearing loss. The Board concluded that the Applicant provided sufficient evidence to maintain the one-fifth pension and found no evidence that the policy was in contravention of the Pension Act. The Applicant challenges the decision arguing that he deserves a higher pension entitlement.The case concerns whether the Veterans Review & Appeal Board erred in considering medical evidence and applying a policy in determining the applicant's entitlement to pension benefits for hearing loss. The applicant argues that the Board failed to consider new medical evidence and that the policy is in contravention of the Pension Act. The respondent argues that the Board's decision is consistent with the Veterans Review & Appeal Board Act and that the additional evidence submitted by the applicant confirmed the Board's findings.
