Ms. Inetide Gilot seeks judicial review of a decision of the Refugee Protection Division of the Immigration and Refugee Board that she is not a Convention refugee or person in need of protection. She challenges the Board's credibility findings and argues that it failed to properly address her fear of violence and rape in her home country based on gender. Ms. Gilot is a 63-year-old citizen of Haiti who traveled to Canada to seek refugee protection two months after arriving in the country.The Board has questioned the credibility of Ms. Gilot's story that she her family and her cousin were attacked by thieves in 2010 resulting in the death of her cousin. The Board found inconsistencies in her testimony and that of her family members and rejected the medical and police reports. The Board doubted Ms. Gilot's allegations regarding her cousin's murder.
