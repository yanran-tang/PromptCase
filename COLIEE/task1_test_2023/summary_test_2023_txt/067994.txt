Fusheng Ma his wife and child were refused permanent residency in Canada by a Visa Officer due to concerns about the cost of special education for their child Jun who had a moderate intellectual disability. The Officer rejected the family's application based on their past use of public education and a perceived lack of intent to pay for private education. The court found that the Officer breached the duty of fairness by not explicitly informing Mr. Ma that their future intentions were still in question and not providing an opportunity to address the concern. The matter was remitted for redetermination.
