This document contains an unedited judgement of a case in Federal Court Beaudry where the applicant did not appear and the respondent was represented by Patricia Deslauriers. The case relates to the consideration of an application for protection under articles 113 161 and 167 of the Law on Immigration and Refugee Protection. The articles lay out requirements for presenting new evidence and factors that determine whether a hearing is required.The applicant was seeking judicial review of a decision to reject their pre-removal risk assessment (PRRA) application. The court dismissed the application finding no error in the Tribunal's assessment of the evidence or risks. The applicant had claimed refugee protection after coming to Canada from Mauritania but had credibility issues regarding their initial claim. They submitted 19 documents in support of their PRRA application but the Tribunal did not find them to be new evidence.
