In this case the court rules that an application regarding the adequacy of reasons for an inadmissibility report must be dismissed. The applicant who worked as an exotic dancer in Canada was convicted of criminal negligence and aggravated assault after infecting her husband with HIV. She is deemed inadmissible for serious criminality under section 44 of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act.The case involves an applicant who has been found inadmissible due to serious criminality defined by a term of imprisonment of at least two years. The officer conducted an interview with the applicant to assess her circumstances and received written submissions from the applicant's counsel regarding her history and hardships. Despite showing remorse and a desire to remain in Canada the officer recommended the applicant be referred to a disability hearing and placed in detention awaiting an admissibility hearing.
