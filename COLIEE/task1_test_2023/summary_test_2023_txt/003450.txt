Ahmed Ali his wife and son were denied refugee status by the Convention Refugee Determination Division of the Immigration and Refugee Board. Ali claims he was attacked and assaulted by militant Sepah-Sahaba members after delivering a speech condemning their actions. The board found Ali lacked credibility due to inconsistencies in his narrative regarding his employment and the implausibility of his speech being broadcast over outdoor speakers. Justice Russell found the board had committed a reviewable error in its credibility findings and ordered a redetermination by a differently constituted panel.
