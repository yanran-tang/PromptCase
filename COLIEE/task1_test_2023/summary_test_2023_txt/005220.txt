This case involves a judicial review of a decision by an Immigration Officer to deny the applicants' request to remain in Canada on humanitarian or compassionate grounds. The applicants a married couple and their two children had arrived in Canada from Poland and claimed Convention refugee status which was later denied. They then applied for landed immigrant status from within Canada and were denied. The judicial review only considered evidence that was before the decision-maker and not new evidence submitted after the decision.The Lemiecha family's case is being reviewed to determine if they have solid humanitarian and compassionate grounds to remain in Canada despite their home country not being considered dangerous. The review includes information on the family's integration into the Canadian community and their importance to it. The family has submitted letters expressing their desire to remain in Canada as well as certificates letters of support and a job offer. However a medical update for the son Rafal was delayed but ultimately revealed a history of hospitalization for asthma in Poland.
