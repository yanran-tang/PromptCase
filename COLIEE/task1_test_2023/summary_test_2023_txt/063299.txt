This is a case of a Sri Lankan citizen who applied for refugee protection in Canada under sections 96 and 97 of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act. The Refugee Protection Division of the Immigration and Refugee Board denied his application stating that he did not meet the requirements to be deemed a Convention refugee or a person in need of protection. The applicant claimed that he faced persecution in Sri Lanka due to his Tamil ethnicity his marriage to a Sinhalese woman and false accusations of aiding the Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE). He also alleged that he was beaten and threatened by the police and a paramilitary group.ICANT a Tamil seeking asylum in Canada had his Section 96 and Section 97 claims rejected by the RPD due to a lack of credibility and subjective fear as well as the absence of serious harm amounting to persecution. The RPD noted that while the police had a lawful right to detain and question ICANT his treatment did not constitute persecution and his fear could not have been so serious if he had the option of shopping for where he wanted to go.
