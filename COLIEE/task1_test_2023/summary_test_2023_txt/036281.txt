The case involves an application for judicial review of a decision by the Convention Refugee Determination Division of the Immigration and Refugee Board which ruled that the applicant was not a Convention Refugee. The applicant a Hindu minority from Bangladesh claimed that he was persecuted by a local fundamentalist Muslim leader and his followers because of his religion political opinion and membership in a social group. The decision was upheld as the CRDD found issues of credibility and the evidence did not support the claim that state protection was unavailable.
