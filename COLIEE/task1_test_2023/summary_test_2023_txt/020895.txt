The case of Rosa Ines Garcia Garcia and <FRAGMENT_SUPPRESSED> against the Refugee Protection Division of the Immigration and Refugee Board was dismissed in a Federal Court judgment as the applicants did not demonstrate a well-founded fear of persecution for reasons of race religion nationality membership in a particular social group or political opinion under sections 96 and 97 of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act. The court found that the applicants' fear of persecution was not well-founded in an objective sense and they did not demonstrate a serious and objective possibility of being persecuted should they return to Colombia.
