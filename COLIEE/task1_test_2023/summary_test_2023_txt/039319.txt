Ngoc Quang Nguyen a permanent resident of Canada who was born in a Hong Kong refugee camp appealed his removal order from Canada after being convicted of several counts of breaking and entering and mischief. The Immigration Appeal Division (IAD) of the Immigration and Refugee Board of Canada dismissed his appeal citing his criminal record as serious criminality under Canadian law. The Federal Court of Canada upheld the decision stating that the IAD did not make any reviewable errors in interpreting the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act.
