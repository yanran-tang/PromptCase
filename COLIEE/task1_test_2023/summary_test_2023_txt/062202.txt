In the case of Capital Insurance Company v. Dubois a construction contract between Larocque and Simon Dubois Inc. required Larocque to insure his property during the construction process. The insurance company indemnified Larocque for damages and sought to sue the construction company for negligence which the court allowed. The rules of good seamanship and ship navigation are fact-specific and may vary depending on circumstances while the burden of proving a ship's unseaworthiness falls on the shipper. The common law rules regarding ship repairs and maintenance are also important in maritime law.
