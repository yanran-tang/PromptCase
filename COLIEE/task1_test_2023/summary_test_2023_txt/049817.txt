This case is an application for judicial review of a decision by the Canadian Human Rights Commission dismissing the applicant's human rights complaint. The applicant an employee of the Royal Bank of Canada was terminated for misappropriation of funds or kiting. The applicant filed a complaint alleging differential treatment based on her race which was dismissed by the Commission as her termination was for non-discriminatory reasons.Ms. Cowan filed a complaint with the Canadian Human Rights Commission alleging that she was unfairly dismissed by the Royal Bank of Canada. An investigation revealed that the bank considered her conduct to be grounds for immediate dismissal under their code of conduct. The investigator used the definition of kiting from Black's Law Dictionary to describe her activities which included advancing funds from her Visa to pay off her line of credit and transferring funds between accounts to save interest. The complaint was ultimately dismissed by the Commission.
