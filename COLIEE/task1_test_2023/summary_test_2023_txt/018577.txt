The case involves a Kurdish citizen of Turkey who applied for refugee status in Canada in 2005 claiming fear of persecution based on his ethnicity religion and political opinion. He stated that he had been mistreated by authorities and discriminated against in education and other institutions. However the decision of the Refugee Protection Division (RPD) held that he did not have sufficient reason to fear persecution and denied his claim. The case is under judicial review.The case involves an applicant who claimed refugee status in Canada due to alleged persecution in Turkey because of his Kurdish ethnicity and Alevi religious beliefs. The Refugee Protection Division (RPD) rejected the claim finding the applicant not credible and that he did not have a subjective fear of persecution nor was his detention for convention reasons. The applicant appealed arguing errors in the RPD member's decision which were reviewed under reasonableness and correctness standards.
