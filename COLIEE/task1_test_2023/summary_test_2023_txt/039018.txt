A family of Peruvian citizens who had their claims for refugee protection rejected due to complicity in crimes against humanity perpetrated by the Peruvian Navy are challenging the decision. The Immigration and Refugee Board's decision to exclude the family members due to the actions of Mr. Marinas a former member of the Navy was found to be in error and the decision was set aside. The Federal Court of Canada ruled that the Board had made multiple errors in its analysis and did not consider evidence that could have supported a different finding.
