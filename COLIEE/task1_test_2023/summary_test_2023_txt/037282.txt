Wenjuan Yu applied for permanent residence in Canada as a librarian but her application was refused by an immigration officer in San Francisco. Yu sought certiorari and mandamus to quash the decision and require the respondents to consider and process her application properly. Her background included studying English at Tsinghua University serving as a librarian there and working as an instructor in English at the Beijing Institute of Physical Education. She also completed in-service training programs for librarians and served as a visiting scholar and research assistant at the University of California Los Angeles.An immigrant seeking permanent residence in Canada was interviewed by an officer at the Canadian Consulate General in San Francisco in 1988. The officer questioned her background as a librarian without a degree in library science and why she wanted to work as a librarian in Canada. Despite her explanation of her qualifications and experience her application was ultimately refused due to not having graduated from a library science program of study.
