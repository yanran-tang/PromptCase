The Kishun family from Guyana consisting of a wealthy businessman his wife and daughter fled to Canada due to being targeted by individuals of African origin intensified by their wealth and living near a violent village. Despite finding the family credible the Refugee Protection Division of the Immigration and Refugee Board determined they were not Convention refugees or in need of international protection. The Federal Court dismissed their application for judicial review citing the panel's findings were reasonable and that the Kishuns would not face persecution or risk of cruel and unusual treatment if they were to return to Guyana.
