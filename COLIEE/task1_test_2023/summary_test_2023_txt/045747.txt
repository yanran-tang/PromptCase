The case raises questions about the principles applied in interlocutory injunctions specifically whether there is an obligation to establish a strong prima facie case or whether the court must only ensure the action is not frivolous or vexatious. Lord Addy considers the impact on the defendant's business and the preservation of the status quo.Lord Diplock's speech suggests that in interlocutory injunction proceedings it is not necessary to establish a strong or even prima facie case. He also criticizes the overruling of trial judges' decisions and emphasizes the importance of their expertise in the subject matter. However some argue that his statements may be considered mere obiter dictum and not applicable to all cases.
