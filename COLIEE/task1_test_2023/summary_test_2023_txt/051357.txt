The case involves an application for judicial review of a decision by the Refugee Protection Division of the Immigration and Refugee Board that the applicant was not a Convention refugee nor a person in need of protection due to lack of credibility. The applicant a citizen of China had fled to Canada after being sought by the Chinese authorities for writing leaflets for Falun Gong practitioners. The Federal Court ultimately dismissed the application for judicial review finding that the Board's decision was sound and unassailable on a reasonableness standard.
