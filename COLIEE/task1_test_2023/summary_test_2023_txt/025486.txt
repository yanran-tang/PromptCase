This unedited federal court case involves Daniel Gratini Santana and another individual who have applied for asylum in Canada but have not provided the essential elements to support their claim under the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act. The Act defines a refugee as someone who has a well-founded fear of persecution due to their race religion nationality membership in a social group or political opinions while a person in need of protection is someone who would be at risk of torture cruel treatment or punishment or threat to life if returned to their country of origin.The applicants spouses and citizens of the Dominican Republic sought refugee protection in Canada due to persecution by the female applicant's father who pressured them to end their interracial relationship and physically attacked the male applicant. The female applicant was forced to have an abortion and they eventually fled to Canada. The Board rejected their claim citing lack of credibility. The Court upheld the decision as there was no evidence of persecution on a Convention ground.
