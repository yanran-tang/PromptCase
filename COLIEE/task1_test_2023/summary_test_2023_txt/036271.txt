This case involves an appeal by Red Coast Imports Inc. from a decision to strike out its statement of defense due to its failure to report and pay private copying levies to the Canadian Private Copying Collective. CPCC also sought payment of the statutory penalty an accounting and powers of audit. Red Coast failed to provide an accurate affidavit of documents leading to the striking out of its defense.A summary of a legal case in Canada involving the Canadian Private Copying Collective (CPCC) and Red Coast in which the defendant was required to provide a complete and accurate affidavit of documents by a certain date but failed to do so. The CPCC had to file a second motion to compel the affidavit and seek other remedies under Rule 227. The defendant's lawyer requested a delay until his client's return to Montreal and arrangements were made for a discovery session.
