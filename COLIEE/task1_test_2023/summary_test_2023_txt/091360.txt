This is a judicial review of a decision by the Refugee Protection Division stating that Valentina Petrova a citizen of Moldova was not a Convention refugee or a person in need of protection. The Board found her fear of persecution not credible due to inconsistencies and insufficient evidence that she was targeted. The Applicant argues that the board misconstrued evidence made unreasonable findings and erred in assessing credibility.t argues that the Board's analysis of her credibility fails to meet the legal standards. She claims that the Board did not provide adequate and real reasons for its findings and that its conclusions were based on implausibilities and speculation. The Applicant notes that the Board did not provide specific reasons for its finding that she was vague or for rejecting her evidence of attacks. She argues that the Board should have made clear findings of credibility and justified them with reference to the evidence.
