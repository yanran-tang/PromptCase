This is an application for judicial review of a decision by a Pre-Removal Risk Assessment officer who concluded that two applicants were not Convention refugees nor persons in need of protection. The applicants a widow and her son from India had previously made a claim for refugee status in Canada which was refused. They now allege that they will be at risk if deported back to India.ed decision is reasonable based on the evidence before the PRRA Officer the Court should not interfere with the decision even if another decision could have been made. The Court finds that the PRRA Officer did not make any patently unreasonable error or based his decision on erroneous findings of fact. The Court also concludes that the PRRA Officer considered all the personal evidence submitted by the applicants and the decision does not violate the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms or the United Nations Convention Against Torture and Other Cruel Inhuman or Degrading Treatment or Punishment.
