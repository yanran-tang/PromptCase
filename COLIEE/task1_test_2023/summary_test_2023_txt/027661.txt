This case involves an application for judicial review of a decision to refuse a religious worker permit exemption and temporary resident visa. The applicant a pastor of a church in Ghana had been invited to work as an associate minister in Toronto for two months but was denied entry by a visa officer who did not believe he would leave Canada at the end of his authorized stay. The applicant's application was rejected and the offer of employment had lapsed at the time of the hearing.The applicant was denied a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) for Canada as the officer believed that he had economic incentive to remain in Canada and not return to his home country of Ghana. Factors taken into consideration included his employment ties to Ghana lack of evidence of previous international travel and large deposits in his bank account that the officer believed were not genuine. The applicant is now seeking a review of the decision.
