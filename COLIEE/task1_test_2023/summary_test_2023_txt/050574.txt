This is a judicial review of a decision made by an Immigration Officer that denied the applicants humanitarian or compassionate grounds to remain in Canada. The applicants a married couple and their children arrived in Canada from Poland in 1989 seeking Convention refugee status which was later denied. The application for judicial review was granted but a preliminary issue regarding the admissibility of evidence post-dating the decision was raised and ultimately dismissed.The Lemiecha family's case is being reviewed by the recipient's office to determine whether there are any humanitarian and compassionate grounds for them to remain in Canada. The family who are from a country deemed safe to return to by the Ottawa Office have submitted letters expressing their desire to stay as well as letters of support from various members of their community. However a medical update for one of their sons was delayed and not promptly forwarded to the Immigration Centre.
