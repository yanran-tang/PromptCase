This is a judicial review of a decision by Canada's Refugee Protection Division refusing a Mexican citizen's request for refugee protection. The applicant sought protection due to an abusive relationship with a senior government official. The court found that the board did not properly consider the applicant's personal situation in relation to the abuser and did not properly consider whether the applicant had a viable internal flight alternative in Mexico.The applicant a Mexican national fled to Canada in 2005 seeking refugee protection after being physically and sexually abused by a public official in her home state of Hidalgo. The Board rejected her claim on the grounds that the Federal District of Mexico City was providing adequate protection to victims of domestic violence. The Federal Court reviewed the decision and found that the Board erred in its application of the law and ordered a new hearing.
