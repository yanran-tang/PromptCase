The Federal Court dismissed an application for judicial review of a decision by the Immigration and Refugee Board wherein the applicant was found not to be a Convention refugee or a person in need of protection. The applicant a citizen of Saint Vincent had fled to Canada after suffering abuse at the hands of her ex-husband who was arrested and charged for assaulting her. The Board found that state protection was available to the applicant and the Court found that the Board's decision was reasonable and fell within an acceptable range of outcomes based on the facts and law.
