This is an application for judicial review of a decision of the Refugee Protection Division of the Immigration and Refugee Board. The principal applicant his wife and their two adult children are citizens of the Philippines. They claimed to be Convention refugees or persons in need of protection as they faced persecution by the police due to their refusal to pay protection money. However the Board concluded that their claims have no nexus to a Convention refugee ground as they do not face a personalized risk to their lives and rejected their claims.The applicants a family from the Philippines claimed refugee status in Canada due to being targeted by corrupt police officers who sexually harassed and extorted them. The Board rejected their claim stating that the harm they faced did not constitute persecution on a Convention ground and that they faced only a generalized risk of persecution. The Board also found that they did not face a personal risk warranting protection under section 97 of the Act.
