Hung Pong Man a citizen of China who arrived in Canada in 1988 as an undocumented arrival at Vancouver International Airport was ordered to be deported from Canada after being convicted of several crimes. In this application for judicial review he argued that he faced significant risk of being tortured or executed by Chinese government authorities and that a risk assessment had not been performed prior to his removal. The court granted the application and ordered that a risk assessment be conducted before any removal from Canada takes place.
