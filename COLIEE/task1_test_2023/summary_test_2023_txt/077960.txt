The issue in this case was whether an Ontario court order giving custody of the applicant's children to her and prohibiting their removal from Ontario precluded her removal from Canada. The Federal Court determined that the order did not preclude her removal as the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act does not recognize provincial court custody orders as grounds for granting a stay.Ms. Idahosa a refugee claimant who gave birth to two Canadian citizen children in Canada was ordered by the Federal Court to be removed from Canada. However she received an ex parte custody order from a provincial court allowing her to have custody of her children and preventing their removal from the province. Ms. Idahosa argues that the order means she cannot be removed from Canada but the Federal Court must determine if the order has the effect of staying the removal order.
