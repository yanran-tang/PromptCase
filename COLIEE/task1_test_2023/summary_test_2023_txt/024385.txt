The applicants citizens of the Democratic Republic of Congo claimed refugee protection in Canada citing political persecution. However their refugee claim was dismissed by the Refugee Protection Division of the Immigration and Refugee Board which found inconsistencies in their story and lack of credibility. The applicants argued that the RPD failed to conduct a separate analysis under section 97 of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act but the court found that such an analysis was not necessary in this case. The application for judicial review was dismissed.
