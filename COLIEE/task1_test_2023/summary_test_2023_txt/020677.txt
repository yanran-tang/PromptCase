Whirlpool Canada LP appealed a decision by the Trade-marks Opposition Board on behalf of the Registrar of Trade-marks. The decision maintained in part the respondent's SPEED QUEEN trade-mark registration. The sole issue in question was whether the respondent had established use of its trade-mark during the relevant period. Ultimately the appeal was dismissed.The article discusses a case of a trademark dispute involving the registration of the SPEED QUEEN mark in Canada. The decision under review expunged the registration of the mark for all wares and services except laundry washing machines and laundry dryers as the respondent had established use of the mark in association with these wares during the relevant period. The article provides details of the use of the mark and the evidence presented in support of the respondent's claim.
