The applicant had his application for Canadian citizenship denied on the grounds that he had not centralized his life to the extent required by the Citizenship Act. The applicant had spent a period of time studying in California but had since returned to Canada and resided with his family. The Citizenship Judge found that the applicant had not spent enough time in Canada to fulfill the intent of the Act and had not sufficiently centralized his life in Canada.The Citizenship Judge refused an applicant's application for Canadian citizenship due to a shortfall in the number of days the applicant has been present in Canada caused by attending college in California for nine semesters. The Judge considered whether the applicant has centralized their life in Canada and spent enough time there to become part of Canadian society.
