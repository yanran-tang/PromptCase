This legal case involves an application for judicial review dismissed with costs. The respondent argues that they are entitled to enhanced costs under Rule 400(3)(c) (e) and (i) of the Federal Courts Rules including the doubling of costs from the date of an offer to settle that was not accepted. The applicant argues that the scale of costs is res judicata and that there is no basis for enhanced costs since they did not depart from Tariff B and Column III. The assessment officer must consider these arguments before making a decision.counsel the Assessment Officer has the authority to determine the number of units for each item under Column III based on Rule 409's wording and factors in subsection 400(3). They argue that Rule 420's doubling of costs is mandatory unless ordered otherwise by the Court. The Federal Court of Appeal has stated that party-and-party costs should be assessed in accordance with Column III unless modified.
