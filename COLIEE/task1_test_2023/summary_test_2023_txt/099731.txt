The case involves Paulin Ndoja and his family who are citizens of Albania and sought refugee protection in Canada after failing to obtain it in the United States. The Refugee Protection Division of the Immigration and Refugee Board of Canada refused their application for protection concluding that adequate state protection existed in Albania. The court found that the Board's decision was reasonable and dismissed the application for judicial review. The factors taken into consideration were the documentary evidence the credibility of the applicants and the adequacy of the state protection systems in Albania.
