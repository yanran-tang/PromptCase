The case is about damage to an individual's property caused by the operation of the St. Andrew's Dam near Lake Winnipeg. The plaintiff claims for lost property and a home among other things caused by bank erosion of the Red River. The defence argues that the dam is not the cause of the erosion and slumping and suggests a number of other factors. The court must determine if a separate trial of this issue will result in the just expeditious and least expensive determination of the proceeding.The case involves a dispute over damages caused by the operation of St. Andrew's Dam on the Red and Assiniboine Rivers. The plaintiff proposes a separate determination of an issue related to whether the operation of the dam caused erosion and slumping of the river banks. The defendant opposes the motion arguing the issue is irrelevant and unfair. The trial is expected to take five weeks.
