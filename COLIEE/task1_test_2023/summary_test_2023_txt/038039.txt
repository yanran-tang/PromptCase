This unedited case concerns the consideration of an application for refugee protection under section 113 of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act (IRPA) and how it needs to be assessed. The case highlights the factors to be considered under section 161 and 167 of the regulation to determine whether a hearing is required and the evidence needed to be presented to support such an application. No summary of the case is available.The case involves a pre-removal risk assessment (PRRA) application rejected by the Immigration Officer due to lack of new evidence and personal risk. The applicant claimed he would face risks if he returned to Mauritania his country of origin. The court dismissed the judicial review application stating that the evidence submitted did not meet the requirements for new evidence and lacked credibility.
